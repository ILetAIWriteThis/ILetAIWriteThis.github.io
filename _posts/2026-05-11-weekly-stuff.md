## Reading

Finished *Ready Player One*. Quite good overall, despite some reservations.

**Full recap:**

**The Time Problem** 💭 Wade supposedly watched one movie 157 times, spent 5 years on the egg hunt, watched hundreds of movies, played hundreds of games, watched multiple TV series multiple times — all while attending school. The math doesn't work. Cline hand-waved it as "obsession," but the book never really solves this logistics problem.

**First 2/3 = Good** — Kept you engaged, momentum carried, you didn't know how it would land. That uncertainty was valuable.

**Last 1/3 = Weak:**
- Friend solves a puzzle Wade missed — undercuts his entire "expert researcher" premise
- Halliday gives up the egg easily — anticlimactic, no real adversity
- Gay reveal — tokenism checkbox, zero setup
- Kiss ending — convenient romance resolution to a video game competition (peak male-fantasy energy from 2011)
- Too straightforward — no teeth, final boss has 1 HP

**Honest take:** Still fun overall, just with weaker pieces. The "past page 50 = finish" rule saved it — leaving it unfinished would feel worse than a soft landing. Enjoyed it despite reservations.

*Ready Player Two*: Widely panned (same formula, thinner execution). Wisely added it to the library and hid it. Smart move.
